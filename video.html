Video


Artist Statement

There are periods where we don't see or talk to each other and when we do have the time it's not very long. I cherish every moment whenever we spend more than one day with each other. We're close yet so far.


Process and Aesthetic Approach

My original concept completely changed last second to expressing the societal standard of education, rendering students to become busier and busier. The struggle to balance a work, play, social life has become seemingly more difficult, impacting friendships and relationships. The audio is a ukulele cover of the song Unconditionally by Katy Perry and the audio of the clock ticking was recorded separate directly from the actual clock with Adobe Audition. I recorded the ukulele with the software GarageBand, tweaked a little bit of the digital presets for enhancement, not too much though because I want to keep the classic ukulele sound, then I exported it as a wav file. The song Unconditionally was personal choice to play as it reflects my interpretation of the word unconditional and represents meaning to a fairly recent major life event. Next I imported the ukulele and clock wav files into an Adobe Audition multitrack. After adjusting the clock audio I exported both files as one wav file and imported it into Adobe Premiere Pro. From there I edited the video files to blend in the audio. My visual approach for this video was to portray a third person point of view for the most part, since I'm also a major part of the video. Even though there were many shots I wanted to shoot handheld, most of the shots were mounted on a tripod. I also attempted to limit the beginning shots to be more noisy/dark and the second half of shots to be brighter, in order to emphasize my feelings.
